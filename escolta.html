Follow and scan the banners at First United Building and find geolocated pockets of mossy forests in Escolta.

Slate buildings that housed mine workers are now engulfed by moss and twisted tree branches.
FUB Lobby 1
Although the prominant backdrop was Sitka Spruce, they are not native, and the forests doesn’t feel ancient. But it did still feel like a rainforest in areas of heavy moss, and pockets of lichen.
FUB Lobby 2
Leading up to, and around the Black Falls, many types of fern were identified, including harts-tongue, fern, deer fern, polypody fern, male/lady fern, some of which were also growing on the water fall walls. The Waterfall is loud and casts a refreshing mist to us as we pass by. They were black, due to the dark rocks behind the stream and the murky tone of the water created a mysterious and lively black pool.
FUB Lobby 3
We were welcomed by rain, which wasn't too bad under the canopy. Trees with gnarled and twisted branches, their lower branches like shelves for mosses and ferns. Moss hangs from rotting logs over a stream, taking the same shape as the water flowing over some rocks beneath it.
FUB Lobby 4
In keeping with the Mabinogion themed visit to Maentwrog, we visited Coed Felenrhyd, an SSSI, and believed to be the site in which Pryderi was killed in a battle with Gwydion. Moss mosaics in all shades of green, orange, red and brown. Haircap mosses were dressed proudly with delicate water droplets worn on its narrow green spikes, like miniature jewels.

4th floor hallway
After an initial short climb, we descended to a still bog area, silent and delicate. Haircap mosses stand the tallest we’ve ever seen, providing a furry mattress beneath the gnarled canopy. The trees appear to stretch laterally in unison, as if reaching or praying to an unseen force. Forkmoss and feathermoss provide velvety whispers of gold, yellow and beige amidst the emeralds and jades.
