We are an artist duo from the UK and the Philippines. For our new project The Possibility of Forests, we explored mossy forests in the Philippines and in Wales. Philippine mossy 'cloud' forests are high-altitude tropical rainforests that sit on the cloud line of very tall mountains. In Wales, mossy forests are ‘Celtic’ temperate rainforests that enjoy very damp, shady and cool conditions of the British hyperoceanic climate. Cloud forests and Celtic rainforests are wildly different things, but they are both very rare, very sensitive, and very mossy.

We feel like there could be a potent energetic connection between cloud forests and Celtic rainforests, so we want to build two public art installations in Davao and Wales. This year, with a grant from British Council Philippines, we visited cloud forests in the Mount Apo range and Celtic rainforests in Eryri Snowdonia National Park. We were instantly struck by their similarities. Aside from all the moss, these forests are rich with endemic species that thrive in their respective microclimates. But most importantly, they store and release so much clean, soft water to Davao and Wales!

We eased into The Possibility of Forests by starting our journey in Arroceros Forest Park in Manila and exploring Mount Makiling in southern Luzon. Not long after, we plunged into multi-day hikes and wild camping on Mt Apo’s Kapatagan Trail and Mabanlas Peak in the Obu Manuvu ancestral domain. A couple of months later, we drove through the awe-inspiring landscape of Eryri Snowdonia National Park to visit remnant rainforests in UNESCO Dyfi Biosphere Reserve and Meirionnydd Oakwoods Bat Sites and Special Areas of Conservation.

We’ve collected a lot of photos, audio-visual materials, observations and memories on our expeditions, enough to form some concepts for our twin forest installations. We presented our first Field notes from a mossy forest at Stiwdio Maelor in Corris, Wales on August 24th. Now we come back full circle, not far from our first forest site Arroceros Forest Park, to present Field notes from a mossy forest at 98B COLLABoratory in Escolta, Manila.

We've used AR and 360 for the web to bring mossy forests to audiences in interesting ways. Choose from 3 paths: the 98B COLLABoratory Trail is accessible only at First United Building in Escolta, while the 360 Forest Trail and the Exploration Trail can be enjoyed anywhere.
